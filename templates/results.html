Looking at the above table, one thing that immediately jumps out is the second and third rows, both because they have very high training accuracy and because they're exactly the same. We used the same set of test and train data on these two models and after looking at their predictions, realized they were rejecting nearly every song (only a total of two songs were accepted for both). This was a result over overfitting the data. We realized that in taking playlists of over length 20, setting aside 1/3 of the songs to classify as 1 and then sampling 50 songs to classify as 0 was overfitting our models to predict 0 more often. We hypothesized that if we reduced the number of non-playlist songs we were adding we could achieve a more accurate model and we were right!
